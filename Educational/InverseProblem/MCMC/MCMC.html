What your problem looks like:

This approach is applicable to highly nonlinear problems of the form $\textbf{d} = g(\textbf{m})$.


What is the advantage of random sampling?

  • Reduces computational cost
  • Helps converge on a solution when no closed form exists
  • When there are so many model parameters that direct search is impossible
  • Gives you a more global picture. Gives you the change to explore other local minimums (think about non-convex misfits)
  • Allows you to make claims on uncertainty quantification.

When would you NOT want to us MCMC (or randomization)?

When your misfit function is already convex! Why use computationally expensive, random guesses to converge on a solution when you can find the exact answer in real time.
