L2 Norm (Least Squares)


What your problem looks like:

This approach is applicable to discrete, linear, overdetermined problems of the form $\textbf{d} = \textbf{G}\textbf{m}$.

The goal is to minimize the squared prediction error or residual. Minimize:

$\textbf{r} = \textbf{d} - \textbf{G}\textbf{m}$
min $\phi(\textbf{m}) = \textbf{r}^T\textbf{C}_d^{-1}\textbf{r} = (\textbf{d} - \textbf{G}\textbf{m})^T\textbf{C}_d^{-1}(\textbf{d} - \textbf{G}\textbf{m}) $

It turns out the solution is $\textbf{m} = (\textbf{G}^T\textbf{C}_d^{-1}\textbf{G})^{-1}\textbf{G}^T\textbf{C}_d^{-1}\textbf{d}$

This is an unbiased estimator of m (unlike when you add regularization). Downside, you may get solutions to blow up.
